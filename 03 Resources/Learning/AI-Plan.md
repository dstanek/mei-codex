### Foundational Knowledge

1. **Mathematics for AI**: Deepen your understanding of linear algebra, calculus, probability, and statistics. These are foundational for understanding how algorithms learn and make predictions.
    
    - **Resource**: "Mathematics for Machine Learning" by Marc Peter Deisenroth, A Aldo Faisal, and Cheng Soon Ong is a comprehensive resource.
2. **Machine Learning**: Grasp the basics of machine learning, including supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning. Understand different algorithms like decision trees, neural networks, clustering, and regression models.
    
    - **Resource**: "Hands-On Machine Learning with Scikit-Learn, Keras, and TensorFlow" by Aurélien Géron provides practical understanding.
3. **Deep Learning**: Dive into neural networks, understand architectures like CNNs (Convolutional Neural Networks) and RNNs (Recurrent Neural Networks), and explore newer models like Transformers.
    
    - **Resource**: "Deep Learning" by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, and Aaron Courville covers theoretical and practical aspects.

### Specialization in AI

4. **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**: Since you're interested in how AI like GPT works, learning NLP is crucial. This includes text preprocessing, word embeddings, and working with models like BERT and GPT.
    
    - **Resource**: "Natural Language Processing in Action" by Lane, Howard, and Hapke is a great starting point.
5. **Generative Models**: Explore GANs (Generative Adversarial Networks) and Variational Autoencoders (VAEs), leading to a better understanding of how generative models like GPT create content.
    
    - **Resource**: The "Deep Learning Specialization" by Andrew Ng on Coursera touches on these topics.

### Practical Application and Advanced Topics

6. **Frameworks and Tools**: Gain hands-on experience with TensorFlow, PyTorch, and cloud AI services from AWS, Google Cloud, and Azure. Implement projects to solidify your learning.
    
    - **Project Ideas**: Create a text generator, sentiment analysis tool, or a chatbot. Platforms like GitHub offer myriad project examples and open-source code.
7. **Prompt Engineering and Fine-Tuning**: Learn the art of prompt engineering for models like GPT-3. Understand the principles of fine-tuning these models for specific applications.
    
    - **Resource**: The OpenAI documentation and blog posts provide insights into prompt engineering and fine-tuning practices.
8. **Ethics in AI**: Understand the ethical implications of AI, including bias, fairness, and privacy concerns. It's crucial for developing responsible AI solutions.
    
    - **Resource**: "Weapons of Math Destruction" by Cathy O'Neil explores the dark side of big data and algorithms.

### Continuous Learning and Community Engagement

- **Conferences and Workshops**: Attend AI and machine learning conferences such as NeurIPS, ICML, and ACL to stay updated on cutting-edge research.
- **Online Courses and Specializations**: Platforms like Coursera, edX, and Udacity offer specialized courses on AI and machine learning.
- **Research Papers**: Follow arXiv for the latest preprints in AI, machine learning, and deep learning.
- **Join AI Communities**: Engage with communities on Reddit, Stack Overflow, and GitHub to discuss ideas, get feedback, and collaborate on projects.

### Practical Steps

1. **Set Clear Goals**: Define what being an "AI expert" means for you. Is it about publishing research, developing applications, or leading AI projects?
2. **Create a Learning Path**: Based on the areas above, design a structured learning path. Allocate time daily or weekly for learning and projects.
3. **Build and Share Projects**: Apply what you learn by building projects. Sharing these on GitHub or writing about them on a blog can help solidify your knowledge and get feedback from the community.
4. **Seek Mentorship**: Connect with AI professionals and seek mentorship. This can provide guidance and accelerate your learning process.

Becoming an AI expert is a journey of continuous learning and application. Given your programming background and experience in the cloud space, you have a solid foundation to build upon. The key is to remain curious, stay updated with the latest advancements, and engage with the AI community.
